Modified Sierpinski Triangle

This take on the Sierpinski Triangle results in a triangle which follows the sierpinski triangle pattern, but randomizes the color and width of the lines of the triangle. This image was taken during one such run of the triangle, but many different triangles could result due to the various rgb colors and the maximum width of 10 pixels. Here is the code for this triangle: 

    def modify_sierpinski(t,depth,length):
      t.pd()
      t.pensize(random.randrange(0, 10))
      if depth == 1:
        t.lt(60)
        t.fd(length)
        t.rt(120)
        t.fd(length)
        t.rt(120)
        t.fd(length)
        t.rt(180)
      else:
        modify_sierpinski(t, depth - 1, length/2)
        t.fd(length/2)
        t.color(random.randrange(0,256), random.randrange(0,256), random.randrange(0,256))
        modify_sierpinski(t, depth - 1, length / 2)
        t.color(random.randrange(0,256), random.randrange(0,256), random.randrange(0,256))
        t.bk(length / 2)
        t.lt(60)
        t.fd(length / 2)
        t.rt(60)
        modify_sierpinski(t, depth - 1, length / 2)
        t.color(random.randrange(0,256), random.randrange(0,256), random.randrange(0,256))
        t.rt(120)
        t.fd(length / 2)
        t.lt(120)

Modified Tree Fractal

This is a modified tree fractal in which I tried to use the theme of autumn for this tree. This was represented by the two major colors of the tree: brown and orange, the colors trees might be in the fall. I also tried to imitate the fact that a lot of trees have thicker branches the closer you are to the center/bottom of the tree, eventually branching out into the leaves.
